About Me

I am currently a third-year PhD Candidate of Management Information Systems at the University of Georgia, Terry College of Business. I have been working on Theory Research Exchange (T-Rex) project, which focuses on advancing literature review and knowledge analytics. T-Rex proposes to digitize core knowledge in publications, which facilitates developing and applying methods for processing graphical representations of causal and process models to advance theoretical research. I use a variety of methods including network science, graph theory, and natural language processing. I have defended my dissertation proposal and my dissertation chair is Dr. Rick Watson, who oversees the T-Rex project together with Drs. Xia Zhao and Jane Webster.

T-Rex

T-Rex (Theory Research Exchange) aims to improve the process of literature reviewing and knowledge synthesis. T-Rex supports digitally encoding publications' core knowledge contributions and ancillary data via a labeled property graph database. Encoding knowledge into a graph database brings new opportunities to manage and analyze knowledge. There is an application to facilitate the coding process - Codasaurus. Codasaurus automatically generates Cypher code for a graph version of a publication with information input by a researcher. Analysaurus is under development to support querying and analytics.
